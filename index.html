Orchestral Music Overview

The Barrier Reef Orchestra focuses mainly on classical orchestral music, with this type of music originating late into the 16th century, but becoming more popular in the 18th century during the midst of the Baroque and Classical eras. An orchestra can simply be described as as instrumental ensemble which varies in size and composition. The usual 'back-bone' of an orchestra is an ensemble of bowed stringed instruments which is generally accompanied by a series of wind and percussion instruments, typically containing more than one player per section (especially the strings).
